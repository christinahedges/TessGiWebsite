Title: TESS at a glance
Save_as: quicklook-mission.html

[TOC]

The TESS mission has been in operation since 2018 and during its lifetime it has observed both the northern and southern hemispheres.

Each hemisphere is divided up into sectors, where a sector consists of the combined field of view from TESSs four cameras ~ 24 x 98 degrees.

TESS has a simple step and stare strategy, where each sector is observed for approximate 27 days before the mission moves onto the next. 

Below you will find videos indicating the field of view as observed by TESS and how these sectors map out each hemisphere.

TESSs [primary mission](https://heasarc.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/tess/primary.html) lasted two years and ran from July 25th 2018 until July 4th 2020. It imaged both the northern and the southern hemisphere with each hemisphere being split up into 13 sectors. During the primary mission TESS images approximately 70% of the sky. 

The [first extension of the mission](https://heasarc.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/tess/extended.html) lasted an additional two years and began in July 4th 2020 ending on September 1st 2022. At the end of the first extension TESS will have mapped out ~89% of the sky. 

TESS will be entering the [second extension of the mission](https://heasarc.gsfc.nasa.gov/docs/tess/second-extended.html) directly after the first. By the end of the second extension, TESS will have almost completely mapped out the entire sky with a coverage of ~97%.

In the table below we provide basic information about each year of the mission, such as dates, sectors, data products, links to pointing information, and [data release notes (DRNs)](https://archive.stsci.edu/tess/tess_drn.html) provided by the project. These DRNs provide a detailed description of modifications to the data and processing pipeline, in addition to any issues that may have occurred during a sector. 

A condensed version of these data release notes can be found via our [searchable DRN database](data_release_notes.html).
